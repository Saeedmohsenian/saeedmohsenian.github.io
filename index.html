Research Areas

Mechanical Engineering / Heat Transfer / Fluid Dynamics

Core Areas:

  • Multiphase Flow Modeling: Gas-liquid and liquid-solid transport in industrial or biomedical systems; Nanofluid dynamics using Eulerian/Lagrangian models.
  • Thermal Management & Heat Transfer: Passive and active thermal systems for electronics; Microscale and nanoscale heat transfer; Radiative and convective transport.
  • Vortex Dynamics & Transient Fluid Mechanics: Unsteady, high-energy-density flow phenomena; Pulsatile or oscillatory flows for mixing and transport.
  • CFD & Optimization: Turbulence modeling (RANS/LES), non-Newtonian flow; Design optimization using metaheuristics; Inverse problems.
  • Energy Systems: Solar energy systems and thermal storage; Waste heat recovery and optimization of renewable energy devices.
  • Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD): Nano- and microfluidic systems subjected to magnetic/electric fields.
  • Advanced Cooling Techniques: Heat sink design with nanofluids; Phase-change materials and microchannel cooling.

Biomedical Engineering / Biotransport / Soft Tissue Mechanics

Core Areas:

  • CSF Hydrodynamics and Brain Biomechanics: Simulation of CSF flow in normal and pathological states; Brain-tissue deformation and intracranial pressure dynamics.
  • Image-Based Modeling: 3D reconstruction of anatomical regions from MRI data for personalized FSI and transport simulations.
  • Soft Tissue Biomechanics: Modeling of deformation, viscoelasticity, and mechanical stress in soft tissues; Host-biomaterial interaction.
  • Biotransport Phenomena: Diffusion and convection of therapeutic agents through porous and deforming biological tissues.
  • Fluid-Structure Interaction (FSI): Coupling between flow and tissue deformation in cardiovascular or central nervous systems.
  • Computational Modeling of Medical Devices: CFD-driven design and analysis of injection needles, catheters, and implants.

Cross-Disciplinary Themes / Emerging Areas

Core Areas:

  • Multiphysics Coupling: Integrated modeling of heat, mass, momentum, and tissue mechanics in complex systems.
  • Experimental-Computational Hybrid Methodologies: Validating CFD results with MRI, ultrasound, or laboratory data to create predictive digital twins.
