About Me

I am a recent M.S. Bioengineering graduate from Northeastern University with a strong background in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), thermal analysis, and biomechanical system modeling. My experience spans both academic and industry settings, including a one-year internship at Biogen where I built CFD models of CNS drug dispersion.

My work integrates advanced CAD modeling, numerical simulation, and multidisciplinary problem-solving in biomedical and mechanical systems. I’ve used tools such as Ansys Fluent, Star-CCM+, SolidWorks, and MATLAB to address complex challenges in CSF hydrodynamics, brain biomechanics, thermal systems, and drug delivery optimization.

Research Areas

  • Multiphase Flow Modeling and Simulation: Gas-liquid/solid transport, nanofluid dynamics, two-phase flows.
  • Thermal Management & Heat Transfer: Microscale systems, fins, radiators, and solar thermal transport.
  • Vortex Dynamics & Transient Flow: Pulsatile, unsteady flows in confined/biomedical systems.
  • CFD & Optimization: Turbulence modeling, non-Newtonian flow, metaheuristic design optimization.
  • Energy Systems & Sustainability: Thermal storage, waste heat recovery, solar energy devices.
  • MHD & Electrically-Driven Flow: Microfluidic systems using magnetic and electric fields.
  • Advanced Cooling Techniques: Nanofluid heat sinks, phase change cooling, microchannel design.
  • CSF Hydrodynamics & Brain Biomechanics: Simulation of CSF flow, brain deformation, drug delivery.
  • Image-Based Tissue Modeling: MRI-based reconstruction, anatomical modeling using ITK-SNAP.
  • Soft Tissue Biomechanics: Stress/strain modeling in tissues, implant-tissue fluid interactions.
  • Biotransport Phenomena: Diffusion/convection in porous and deformable biological media.
  • Fluid-Structure Interaction: FSI in the cardiovascular/neurological system and implants.
  • Medical Device Modeling: CFD analysis of catheters, implants, and therapeutic delivery tools.
  • Biomedical Nanofluidics: Microchannel drug delivery, nanofiber scaffolds for tissue engineering.
  • Multiphysics Coupling: Heat, mass, momentum, and tissue mechanics in complex systems.
  • Hybrid Experimental-CFD: Validation of simulations using MRI, ultrasound, or lab data.
